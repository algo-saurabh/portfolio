Grey Orange is a warehouse automation company. It provides both software - called as GreyMatter well as the hardware - the Robots built together which is required to automate the warehouse in way that it can meet current fulfilment demands. My project was to facilitate returns management using the Grey orange robotic solution.

Objective

To design an interface that facilitates returns management by enabling returns management operators to seamlessly send and receive returned items using Ranger MoveSmart robots.

My Contribution

Discover

What is Returns Management

The returns management process is used in both retail and e-commerce industries and involves processing returned products by collecting, organizing and restocking returned inventory or exchanged items.Following the return of the item, depending on the product and the situation, it must also be decided whether to recycle the item and save useful parts, repair or refurbish it, resell it, or destroy it.

‍

Need for for well crafted Returns Management

With the continuous growth of omnichannel commerce, direct store shipments, and direct home shipments, as well as the complexity of global sourcing, delivery errors are becoming more prevalent. A well-crafted returns management system has a direct impact on a company’s cost of doing business and on the quality of customer service it offers. Improving your returns process can have a positive effect on sales, revenue, and customer retention.

A survey sponsored by Shipping technology leader, ENSICA, revealed these insights about US shoppers:

  • 12% of consumers want a swift refund or credit
  • 67% of shoppers will check your return policy before buying from you
  • 72% of shoppers are willing to spend more and buy more frequently from brands that make the return experience simple

Understanding Ranger MoveSmart Robot

Ranger MoveSmart bot consists of two conveyors mounted on it which carries the packets. It moves freely without any guide wires or optical guide strips and instead uses barcodes on the warehouse floor for route navigation. It creates a simple 2D map of the surroundings for location identification and moves on a path communicated by the GrayMatter.

Stakeholder interview insights

  • The interface should be generic but designed in such a way that it can be tailored to specific use cases
  • Since the operators are people with very minimal educational qualification the design should be very simple and to the point
  • Since the user would be using interface while performing some physical activity so their dependence on interface should be minimal
  • The interface is only to facilitate movement of items and should not interfere with other operations of returns management

Insights from wireframe testing

To validate the wireframes created, I simulated the same environment and tested the wireframes with team by doing moderated usability testing. I also talked to product manager and senior product manager to gather their inputs.

Following are the insights from the testing and interviews done-While testing, we realised that-

  • Operator would find it difficult to perform these actions using the hand held device as they would also be handling the items simultaneously, so there will be chances of device getting misplaced.
  • Handheld device has very less screen space making it difficult to effectively show everything required especially in the flow of placing items on the bot.
  • Information architecture needs to redesigned and no. of steps should be reduced by making it direct action based

Based on these insights, we decide to change the device to mounted tablet as -

  • It would not interface with operators flow and won't get misplaced,
  • Provide with more screen space for better visual representation and quicker actions

Future Scope

  • There is scope to take interviews with actual users.
  • The project can be made even simpler by including different profiles in the design and actions related to them are only shown. Once we design the system for a real client this possibility can be made real.
  • Different types of exceptions that might come can be studied and flow tailored to each type of exception can be designed.
  • Lastly, the step of calling the bot can also be made automatic in certain user profiles as soon as they add location to the item, thus saving time.
