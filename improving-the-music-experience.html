Designing an experimental learning platform from scratch

Duration:

2 Months

Team:

Tanya, Jyotirmoy Boral, Divyanshu Deyalla

Role:

User Research, Competitive Analysis, User Journey, UX/UI Design, Prototyping, Interactions.

Habbit is a platform that connects its users with relevant mentors/experts for them to learn hobbies and skills through habit-based communities with a focus on experiential education where individuals can learn, interact and create.

Objective

To create mobile application from scratch for learners of habbit- where they discover new hobbies, learn them in a way they are comfortable, share their creation within their community and also keep practicing the skill to actually master it.

Approach

My Contribution

During the research phase, I conducted a literature review on e-learning application and semi-structured interviews with potential users and existing users. During the ideation phase, two of my suggested services were included in the final deliverable . I also contributed to the information architecture design, and was responsible for creating the wireframes for the app. Finally, I moderated several usability testing sessions, and led the discussion of deriving design implications from the data with my teammates.

Design Outcome

Design Process

We started the project having a vague idea that we hope to help kids and young adults to learn new skills through interactive learning platform. However, based on our primary research and stakeholders interviews we focussed on anyone who wanted to learn a new skill or upgrade in an existing one. Let's dive deeper into each aspect of the project -

Domain Research

To better understand the problem space, we conducted a literature review and a competitive analysis.For the competitive analysis, we focused on the existing e-learning platforms for skill development.

‍

‍

Our main findings include:

  • Platforms enabling students to meet relevant mentors in online mode is gaining more acceptance
  • Access to free content on the platform to provide an experience is driving adoption to freemium model, resulting in increase in paid subscription on these platforms.
  • Different users preferred different way of learning- some within a group, some individually and some with just their friends
  • Kids report higher satisfaction to video based content. Around 50% of online users indicate preference for video based content on these platform
  • The decision to purchase online solution is influenced by stakeholders such as parents, peers, faculty and content available on internet
  • Peer review and feedback play a key role in discovery and selection of platform which is why these platforms have efficient feedback mechanism

The results motivated us to:

  • To design application for Live interactive class
  • To give trial course for free to give them the first hand experience of the product .
  • Having different services according to the needs of the user
  • Designing a effective and efficient feedback mechanism

Usability Testing

The aims of our evaluation sessions are 1) to identify usability problems with our current design and 2) to assess the learnability of the application.

We conducted our user testing through maze, which provided us with detailed insights regarding our designs.

‍

‍

Observed issues and suggestions from users
  • Overview of the session should be to the point
  • The button Practice should have indication that it will provide more details
  • Service tabs can be made a little more visible

These changes are incorporated and already depicted in the "Major Screens".
